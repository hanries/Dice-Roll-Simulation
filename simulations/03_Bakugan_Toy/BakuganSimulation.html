How this simulation works

Each round, two independent Exp(1) random variables are drawn — one per machine. The smaller one wins that toy slot, labelled B (Bakugan) or Y (Beyblade). This repeats until either the 3rd B or 2nd Y appears.

To generate an Exp(1) random variable in JavaScript, we use the inverse transform method: -Math.log(1 - Math.random()). This works because if U ~ Uniform(0,1), then -log(U) ~ Exp(1).

As you run more simulations, the estimated probability converges to the true answer of 5/16 = 0.3125 by the Law of Large Numbers.
